# Car Insurance Policy

Document ID: POL-CAR-001
Product: ClaimWise Comprehensive Motor Protection
Effective date: 2026-01-01
Currency: EUR

## Coverage Overview

The ClaimWise Comprehensive Motor Protection policy provides coverage for selected motor vehicle incidents involving the insured vehicle. Coverage may include collision damage, third-party liability, theft, vandalism, and approved emergency towing.

The insured vehicle must be listed on the policy schedule at the time of the incident. The driver must be legally permitted to drive and must be either the named policyholder or an authorized driver under the policy terms.

Claims are assessed using the incident description, submitted documents, policy rules, and repair evidence. Some claims may be eligible for automated document checks, but liability decisions and complex accidents may require manual review.

## Collision Coverage

Collision coverage applies when the insured vehicle is damaged in an accident with another vehicle, object, or road hazard. The policy may reimburse approved repair costs after inspection or after receipt of a valid repair invoice.

Required documents for car accident claims:

- accident report
- photos of vehicle damage
- repair invoice or repair estimate
- driver's license of the person driving
- vehicle registration document
- third-party details when another vehicle or person is involved

If police attended the scene, the police reference number should also be submitted. If the accident involved injury, suspected intoxication, or disputed liability, the claim must be routed to manual review.

## Third-Party Liability

Third-party liability coverage may apply when the policyholder is legally responsible for damage to another person's vehicle, property, or bodily injury. Liability is not automatically accepted based only on the policyholder's statement.

Required documents for third-party liability claims:

- accident report
- contact and insurance details of the third party
- photos of the scene and damage
- witness statement when available
- police report when required by law

All third-party injury claims must be manually reviewed. The assistant should not state that liability has been accepted unless a claim handler has confirmed it.

## Theft and Vandalism

Theft coverage applies when the insured vehicle is stolen or when parts of the insured vehicle are stolen. Vandalism coverage applies when the insured vehicle is intentionally damaged by an unknown third party.

Required documents for theft claims:

- police report
- vehicle registration document
- proof of ownership
- all available vehicle keys
- photos when the vehicle is recovered with damage

Required documents for vandalism claims:

- police report
- photos of damage
- repair invoice or repair estimate
- vehicle registration document

Claims involving stolen personal items inside the car are not covered unless the policy schedule includes personal belongings coverage.

## Emergency Towing

Emergency towing may be reimbursed when the insured vehicle cannot be safely driven after a covered accident. Towing must be reasonable and related to the covered incident.

Required documents for towing reimbursement:

- towing invoice
- accident report or breakdown report
- proof of payment

Towing after mechanical breakdown is not covered unless breakdown assistance is included in the policy schedule.

## Exclusions

The following situations are excluded:

- intentional damage caused by the policyholder or authorized driver
- damage while the driver was under the influence of alcohol or drugs
- racing, speed trials, or illegal driving activity
- use of the vehicle for paid transport without commercial coverage
- damage from normal wear and tear
- mechanical failure unrelated to an insured incident
- driving without a valid license
- false or altered documents

## Required Documents Summary

Car accident claims require accident report, photos, repair invoice or estimate, driver's license, and vehicle registration.

Vehicle theft claims require police report, vehicle registration, proof of ownership, and all available keys.

Vandalism claims require police report, photos, repair invoice or estimate, and vehicle registration.

Emergency towing claims require towing invoice, incident evidence, and proof of payment.

## Manual Review Triggers

A car insurance claim must be manually reviewed when:

- the driver was not listed or authorized on the policy
- injury occurred
- liability is disputed
- police report is missing for theft or vandalism
- claimed amount is unusually high
- documents conflict with the incident description
- fraud indicators or altered documents are suspected
