# Home Insurance Policy

Document ID: POL-HOME-001
Product: ClaimWise Home Protection Plus
Effective date: 2026-01-01
Currency: EUR

## Coverage Overview

The ClaimWise Home Protection Plus policy provides coverage for selected damage to the insured home and covered household contents. Covered events may include sudden water damage, fire damage, burglary, storm damage, and approved emergency repair.

The insured address must match the address on the policy schedule. The policyholder must take reasonable steps to prevent further damage after an incident, such as shutting off water, contacting emergency services, or arranging temporary repairs when necessary.

Claims are reviewed based on the type of incident, timing of declaration, supporting documents, exclusions, and reimbursement rules.

## Water Damage

Water damage coverage applies when damage is sudden and accidental. Examples include a burst pipe, accidental overflow from an appliance, or sudden leakage from a fixed water installation.

Home water damage must be declared within 5 days of discovery. If the declaration is submitted after 5 days, the claim must be routed to manual review. Late declaration does not automatically mean rejection, but the claim handler must determine whether the delay worsened the damage or reduced investigation quality.

Required documents for water damage:

- photos of the damage
- damage report from a plumber, contractor, or building manager
- repair invoice or repair estimate
- declaration date and incident date
- proof of emergency repair if emergency mitigation was performed

Gradual leakage, long-term humidity, mold caused by poor maintenance, and damage from unresolved prior issues are excluded.

## Fire Damage

Fire damage coverage applies when the insured home or covered household contents are damaged by accidental fire, smoke, or explosion.

Required documents for fire damage:

- fire department report when emergency services attended
- photos of damage
- repair invoice or estimate
- inventory of damaged contents
- proof of ownership for high-value items

All major fire claims and any claim involving injury must be manually reviewed.

## Theft and Burglary

Theft coverage applies when covered household contents are stolen from the insured address after forcible entry or another covered burglary event.

Required documents for theft and burglary:

- police report
- photos of forced entry or property damage
- inventory of stolen items
- receipts or proof of ownership for valuable items
- repair invoice for damaged locks, doors, or windows

Theft without evidence of forcible entry may require manual review. Lost personal items outside the home are not covered unless the policy includes personal possessions away from home.

## Natural Events

Storm, hail, and certain natural events may be covered when the event directly damages the insured home. The policyholder may be asked to provide weather evidence or a local authority statement.

Required documents for natural event claims:

- photos of damage
- repair invoice or estimate
- date and location of the event
- weather report or local authority notice when available

Damage caused by poor maintenance, pre-existing structural defects, or gradual deterioration is excluded.

## Emergency Repair

Emergency repair may be reimbursed when immediate action is needed to prevent further covered damage. Emergency repair before adjuster review is capped at 500 EUR unless the insurer gives prior approval.

Examples of emergency repair include:

- temporary pipe repair after sudden water leak
- board-up of broken windows after burglary
- emergency lock replacement after forced entry
- temporary roof covering after storm damage

Required documents for emergency repair:

- emergency repair invoice
- proof of payment
- photos before and after repair when available
- explanation of why immediate repair was necessary

## Exclusions

The following situations are excluded:

- gradual leakage or repeated seepage
- mold from poor maintenance
- intentional damage by the policyholder
- normal wear and tear
- pre-existing damage
- pest damage
- renovation defects
- false or altered documents
- damage at an address not listed on the policy

## Required Documents Summary

Water damage claims require photos, damage report, repair invoice or estimate, declaration date, and incident date.

Fire claims require fire department report when applicable, photos, repair estimate or invoice, contents inventory, and proof of ownership for high-value items.

Burglary claims require police report, photos of forced entry, stolen item inventory, receipts or proof of ownership, and repair invoice for damaged entry points.

Natural event claims require photos, repair estimate or invoice, event date and location, and weather or authority evidence when available.

## Manual Review Triggers

A home insurance claim must be manually reviewed when:

- water damage was declared more than 5 days after discovery
- gradual damage may be involved
- the insured address is unclear
- the claim involves high-value contents without proof of ownership
- emergency repair exceeds 500 EUR without prior approval
- documents conflict with the incident description
- fire, injury, or major structural damage is involved
