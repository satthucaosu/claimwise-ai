# Customer FAQ

Document ID: FAQ-CUSTOMER-001
Applies to: ClaimWise Travel, Car, and Home Insurance Customers
Effective date: 2026-01-01

## How long does claim review take?

Most complete claims are reviewed within 5 to 10 business days. A claim is complete when the required information and documents have been submitted and can be read clearly.

Some claims take longer. Manual review may be needed when documents are missing, the claimed amount is high, liability is disputed, policy coverage is unclear, or the incident involves injury or possible fraud indicators.

## Can I submit photos from my phone?

Yes. Phone photos are accepted if they are clear, complete, and readable. Make sure the photo shows the full document or the full damaged area. Avoid blurry images, shadows, glare, and cropped corners.

For damage photos, it is helpful to submit both close-up photos and wider photos that show the surrounding area. For invoices and reports, make sure the provider name, date, amount, and description are visible.

## What happens if I am missing a document?

If a required document is missing, the claim can still be submitted, but review may be delayed. The insurer may ask you to upload the missing document before continuing assessment.

For example:

- a travel delay claim usually needs carrier delay confirmation
- a lost baggage claim usually needs a baggage tag and airline irregularity report
- a car accident claim usually needs an accident report and damage photos
- a home water damage claim usually needs photos, a damage report, and repair evidence

If you cannot obtain a required document, explain why. The claim may be routed to manual review.

## Can I update my claim later?

Yes. You can usually add documents, clarify details, or update your claim after submission. Include your claim ID or policy number when sending additional information.

You should update your claim if:

- you receive a missing report or invoice
- the repair estimate changes
- you discover additional damage
- the airline provides final baggage confirmation
- you entered an incorrect incident date or amount

The assistant should not replace existing facts silently. If new information conflicts with earlier information, the claim may need manual review.

## Do I need original receipts?

Original receipts are preferred because they make reimbursement review faster. If original receipts are unavailable, you may provide alternative proof such as bank statements, order confirmations, invoices, warranty documents, or an itemized list.

Some claims, especially lost baggage and high-value contents claims, may require proof of ownership or purchase value. Claims without receipts may be reimbursed at a reduced value or routed to manual review.

## How do I know if my claim needs manual review?

Manual review may be required when the claim cannot be safely assessed using standard rules. Common reasons include:

- missing required information
- missing or unreadable documents
- late declaration
- high claimed amount
- injury or legal involvement
- disputed liability
- documents that do not match the description
- unclear coverage
- request for an exception

Manual review does not mean your claim is rejected. It means a claim handler needs to examine the details.

## Is a 5-hour travel delay covered?

No. Travel delay coverage applies only when the delay is greater than 6 hours and confirmed by the airline or transport provider. A delay of exactly 6 hours or less is not covered under the standard travel delay rule.

## What documents do I need for lost baggage?

Lost baggage usually requires:

- baggage tag
- airline irregularity report
- police report when theft or suspected theft is involved
- receipts or an itemized list of lost items
- final written confirmation from the airline that the baggage is lost

If you are missing one of these documents, upload what you have and explain what is missing.

## What documents do I need for a car accident claim?

A car accident claim usually requires:

- accident report
- photos of vehicle damage
- repair invoice or repair estimate
- driver's license
- vehicle registration
- third-party details when another person or vehicle is involved

If someone was injured or liability is disputed, the claim will likely need manual review.

## What documents do I need for home water damage?

Home water damage usually requires:

- photos of the damage
- damage report from a plumber, contractor, or building manager
- repair invoice or estimate
- incident date
- declaration date

Water damage should be declared within 5 days of discovery. Late declaration may require manual review.

## Can the assistant approve my claim?

No. The assistant can explain policy rules, identify missing information, summarize likely next steps, and draft customer communications. It cannot approve or reject claims. Final decisions must be made by the insurer or an authorized claim handler.
