# Reimbursement Rules

Document ID: RULES-REIMBURSEMENT-001
Applies to: ClaimWise Travel, Car, and Home Insurance Products
Effective date: 2026-01-01
Currency: EUR

## General Reimbursement Process

Reimbursement is considered only after a claim is submitted with sufficient supporting information and required documents. The customer must show that the expense is related to a covered incident, was actually incurred, and has not already been reimbursed by another party.

The reimbursement process usually follows these steps:

1. claim intake and classification
2. document completeness check
3. coverage review
4. reimbursement amount calculation
5. claim handler approval when required
6. payment to the customer

The assistant may estimate whether documents appear complete, but it must not state that payment is approved unless a final approval status is available.

## Deductibles

Some claims may include a deductible. A deductible is the portion of an approved loss that remains the customer's responsibility.

Typical deductible rules:

- travel delay claims do not have a deductible
- lost baggage may have a 50 EUR deductible
- car collision claims may have a deductible based on the policy schedule
- home water damage may have a 150 EUR deductible

If the deductible amount is not available in the provided context, the assistant should say that the deductible depends on the policy schedule.

## Reimbursement Caps

Standard reimbursement caps include:

- travel delay meals and refreshments: maximum 50 EUR per day
- lost baggage: maximum 1,500 EUR per insured trip
- home emergency repair before adjuster review: maximum 500 EUR
- home water damage reimbursement: subject to policy schedule and repair evidence
- car repair reimbursement: based on approved repair invoice or approved repair estimate

Claims above these caps require manual review. The cap is not a guaranteed payment amount; the reimbursed amount depends on coverage, documentation, exclusions, and approved expenses.

## Travel Reimbursement Rules

Travel delay reimbursement applies only when the delay is greater than 6 hours and confirmed by the carrier. Eligible expenses include reasonable meals, refreshments, and essential local transport during the delay.

Travel delay reimbursement does not cover:

- luxury meals
- alcohol
- unrelated shopping
- missed business opportunity
- inconvenience without receipts
- delay of 6 hours or less

Lost baggage reimbursement may cover documented personal belongings in checked baggage, subject to the 1,500 EUR cap. If receipts are unavailable, the customer should provide an itemized list with estimated purchase dates and values. High-value items may require manual review.

## Car Reimbursement Rules

Car repair reimbursement is based on an approved repair invoice or repair estimate. The insurer may require inspection before payment. Emergency towing may be reimbursed when it is directly related to a covered accident and supported by invoice and proof of payment.

Car reimbursement does not cover:

- unrelated mechanical failure
- maintenance work
- pre-existing damage
- upgrades beyond restoring the vehicle
- damage caused intentionally
- damage while driving illegally

If another party may be liable, the claim may require subrogation review or manual liability assessment.

## Home Reimbursement Rules

Home repair reimbursement is based on damage evidence, contractor report, repair estimate, repair invoice, and policy coverage.

Emergency repair may be reimbursed up to 500 EUR before adjuster review when immediate action was necessary to prevent further covered damage. Amounts above 500 EUR require prior approval or manual review.

Home reimbursement does not cover:

- gradual leakage
- mold from poor maintenance
- pre-existing defects
- cosmetic improvements unrelated to the incident
- repairs at an address not insured by the policy

## Approval Conditions

A reimbursement may proceed only when:

- the claim type is covered by the relevant policy
- required documents have been received and are readable
- the incident date falls within the policy period
- the claimed expense is directly related to the incident
- the claimed amount is supported by receipts, invoices, or approved estimates
- no exclusion clearly applies

If any required document is missing, the assistant should request the missing document before suggesting that reimbursement can proceed.

## Payment Timeline

Once a claim is complete and approved, standard payment is usually issued within 5 to 10 business days. Complex claims, manual review, missing documents, or high-value claims may take longer.

The assistant may explain the general payment timeline but should not provide a specific payment date unless it is available in the claim system.

## Rejection Conditions

A reimbursement may be rejected when:

- the incident is excluded by the policy
- the claim is unsupported by required documents
- documents are false, altered, or inconsistent
- the same expense has already been reimbursed
- the incident occurred outside the policy period
- the customer does not respond to document requests
- the claimed expense is unrelated to the covered incident

When rejection is possible but not confirmed, the assistant should use cautious language and recommend manual review.
